2 Two stages of identity

When Mary opens her photos, two different systems answer two different questions. Step through:

Authentication vs authorization

The split that trips people up
Cognito (the identity provider) knows who Mary is and issues her a token; the load balancer checks that token’s signature on every request. But only the application knows what Mary owns — it runs SELECT * FROM photos WHERE owner='Mary' so she sees her photos and no one else’s. The IdP knows who; the app knows what.

4 Encryption, end to end

Finally, protect the data itself — in motion and at rest:

In motion · at rest

Keep S3 traffic off the internet
The app’s hop to S3 can use a VPC endpoint, so that traffic stays on the private AWS network and never crosses the public internet — even though S3 lives outside your VPC.
Remember

Secure a 3-tier app by segmentation — only the load balancer in the public subnet, everything else private. Identity is two stages: Cognito authenticates and issues a token the load balancer validates, while the application authorizes what data you own. Match each threat to a service: MITM → TLS, DDoS → Shield, SQL injection → WAF, malicious uploads → GuardDuty, vulnerable packages → Inspector, and origin-bypass → locking the load balancer to CloudFront. And encrypt everything — TLS on every hop (with a VPC endpoint to S3) and server-side encryption at rest.
