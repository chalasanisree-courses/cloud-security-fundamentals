2 DLP: is this data allowed to exist here?

Identity checks who logged in, but ignores what they upload. Data Loss Prevention reads the actual content of files — not just the name — looking for regulated patterns. Drop a file on the scanner:

Tools & actions
DLP engines scan for credit-card numbers (PCI), SSNs (PII), medical records (HIPAA), or secret keywords like “Confidential.” On a hit they can quarantine, redact, or alert. Examples: AWS Macie (scans S3), Box Shield (scans a SaaS app).

The SaaS security stack
Three questions, three controls. Identity (OIDC + MFA): are you who you say you are?  DLP: is this data allowed to exist here?  CASB: is this data allowed to leave here? Together they replace the firewall you no longer control.
Remember

Cloud data faces three risks: compliance violations (sensitive data stored where it shouldn’t be), unauthorized access (over-loose sharing), and exfiltration (data leaving for an unmanaged device or cloud). DLP answers “is this data allowed to exist here?” by inspecting file content for PCI/PII/HIPAA patterns and quarantining, redacting, or alerting (AWS Macie, Box Shield). CASB answers “is this data allowed to leave here?” — a traffic cop that blocks downloads to unmanaged devices and revokes cloud-to-cloud OAuth tokens. With identity, they form the SaaS security stack.
