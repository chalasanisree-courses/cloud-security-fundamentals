The pivot
Because defenses eventually fail, the operational question changes. Not “how do we keep hackers out?” but “how quickly can we find them once they’re in?” That mindset — assume breach — is the whole subject of this module.

3 The two clocks

Detection is measured with two stopwatches. The whole goal is to shrink them from months to minutes. Drag the detection speed and watch the damage:

MTTD · MTTR · dwell time

How fast you detect:

One precise definition
Dwell time is the stretch the attacker is inside undetected — from the moment of compromise to the moment you detect them. It lines up with MTTD. Add MTTR (detection → kicked out) and you get the total incident lifecycle, not dwell time. Shrinking dwell time is what limits how much an attacker can steal.
Remember

In the cloud, Identify is undermined by asset sprawl (ephemeral containers, Shadow IT) and Protect by configuration complexity (the open S3 bucket) — so prevention eventually fails and the goal pivots to detection: find them fast. The stack is logs → SIEM (aggregate, store, and correlate) → XDR (AI that links signals across layers, e.g. impossible travel) → SOC analyst (the human who hunts). Success is measured by two clocks: MTTD (time to detect) and MTTR (time to remediate). Dwell time — the undetected stretch — is the compromise-to-detection window (MTTD); shrink it from months to minutes.
