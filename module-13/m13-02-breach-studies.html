1 SolarWinds: poison in a trusted update

The textbook detection failure. The attackers didn’t break in — they were installed, inside software thousands of organizations trusted. Step through it:

A supply-chain attack

What failed: Detect, not Protect
Every preventive control worked — the update was genuinely signed by SolarWinds, so there was nothing malicious for a firewall or antivirus to catch. The failure was detection: the intruders sat undetected for months, moving laterally across thousands of networks, and were found only when a security firm noticed its own compromise. Long dwell time = enormous damage.

The anatomy of a long dwell time

The lesson
You cannot prevent every attack — especially one that arrives inside trusted software. That’s exactly why the detection half of the job matters: assume something will get through, and invest in finding it fast. A short dwell time would have turned a catastrophe into an incident.

3 Once you detect: incident response

Detection is only half of “Threat Detection & Incident Response.” The moment an alert is confirmed, a disciplined response lifecycle kicks in. Click a stage:

The incident-response lifecycle

Remember

SolarWinds was a supply-chain attack — malicious code hidden inside a legitimately-signed software update — and a detection failure, not a prevention one: there was nothing malicious for preventive controls to catch, so the attackers enjoyed a dwell time of months, moving laterally across thousands of networks until a security firm found it by accident. The lesson of the whole course: you can’t prevent everything, so detect fast — and when you do detect, run the incident-response lifecycle: detect & analyze → contain → eradicate → recover → learn. Protect every layer, detect quickly when a layer fails, and respond in a disciplined way.
