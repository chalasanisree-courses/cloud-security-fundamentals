2 Where the vendor's job stops

Every app runs on a stack: infrastructure → platform → application. The three cloud models differ only in how far up that stack the vendor takes over. Click through them — watch the line between your job and theirs move:

Who manages what — the shared responsibility line

You manage & secure Cloud vendor manages & secures
Why this is a security idea
That dividing line is the shared responsibility model. Everything blue is yours to configure and secure. Most cloud breaches happen because a customer assumed the vendor covered something that was actually on the blue side — an open bucket, a missed OS patch.

4 Where it runs

Three deployment choices — and big companies usually pick the third:

On-prem · cloud · hybrid

Remember

Cloud = pay-per-use computing (Uber, not a car; OpEx, not CapEx). The stack — infrastructure, platform, application — sets the three models: IaaS (EC2), PaaS (Heroku, Lambda), SaaS (M365). The vendor/customer split is the shared responsibility model — everything on your side is yours to secure. Deployment is on-prem, cloud, or hybrid.
